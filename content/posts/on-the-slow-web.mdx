The web rewards volume. Algorithms prefer the prolific; engagement metrics
prefer the loud. But the work that lasts — the essays I still re-read years
later, the small personal sites I keep open in pinned tabs — almost always
comes from people who publish less, and publish slower.

## What it costs to publish slowly

The trade is real. You give up:

- the dopamine of frequent posting
- the algorithmic boost of consistency
- the comfortable rhythm of a content calendar

In exchange, you get something harder to measure: each post is a thing you'd
defend in a year. Each post earns its place.

## What I'm trying here

Three rules for this site:

1. Don't publish to a schedule.
2. Don't publish until the idea is at least mostly true.
3. Don't optimise for the homepage; optimise for the one good reader.

We'll see how it goes.
